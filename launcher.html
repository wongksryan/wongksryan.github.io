Boba Launcher — Version 2 (Theoretical Design)

CAD Design

Developed a detailed SolidWorks model exploring pressure chamber geometry, sealing strategies, and projectile guidance under a formal design constraint: a maximum operating pressure of 70 psi while achieving a theoretical launch velocity of at least 50 m/s without structural or projectile failure.

As an additional self-imposed challenge, the design was constrained to use only real off-the-shelf, pressure-rated PVC standards and fittings (with appropriate margins of safety) and commercially available solenoid valves, rather than custom-manufactured components. This constraint strongly influenced geometry, layout, and structural decisions. The project remains a theoretical design and was not physically constructed.

ANSYS Simulation

Structural integrity was evaluated using static load analysis in ANSYS Mechanical to verify stress distributions and deformation under theoretical internal pressure loads. Internal flow behavior and pressure equalization were studied using ANSYS Fluent to estimate theoretical performance and velocity trends.

These simulations were used as comparative design tools in order to understand system behavior and tradeoffs rather than optimizing for construction. It was found that were not a good idea

Putty Launcher — Version 1

Adiabatic Calculations

This project was developed under the constraint of launching a 15 g putty projectile at approximately 40–50 m/s using a maximum operating pressure of 50 psi. Design decisions were guided by component datasheets and material limits to ensure all components remained within their rated operating conditions.

Theoretical performance modeling was based on adiabatic expansion of a diatomic gas, justified by experimental pressure measurements indicating characteristic expansion times on the order of 75–150 ms. This assumption allowed closed-form energy and pressure relationships to be derived without requiring calculus-based numerical methods, which was appropriate to my mathematical background at the time.
