CAD & Analysis

Planning & Measurements

Defined biomechanical constraints, applied anthropometric datasets, and mapped mechanical interfaces to ensure ergonomic fit across a wide range of body sizes.

CAD Version 1

Early exploratory model used to validate overall architecture, kinematics, and packaging of a 2-DOF hip mechanism. Design was intentionally unoptimized, with limited consideration for mass, stress distribution, or manufacturability.

ANSYS & SolidWorks FEA

Conducted structural simulations to validate load paths and identify stress concentrations, informing major geometry changes and design decisions between early and refined iterations.

CAD Version 2 (Post-Review)

Heavily refined design with significant optimization to mass, structural efficiency, and load paths. Geometry was redesigned around real manufacturing constraints, resulting in a configuration that is now fully machinable while maintaining functional and ergonomic requirements.

Manufacturing (In Progress)

CNC Machining

Manufacturing planning underway; components are being designed around achievable tolerances and practical fixturing constraints.

Lathe Work

Future fabrication will include simple turned components such as spacers and shafts once final dimensions are locked.

Manual Milling

Prototyping strategies are being evaluated; fabrication will proceed once the design reaches full design freeze.

Final Assembly

System integration and validation will begin after fabrication of the finalized mechanical components.
