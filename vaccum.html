A modular swerve-drive robotics platform showcasing mechanical design, structural analysis, and integrated hardware/software systems.

CAD

Frame

The swerve module frame was designed with a focus on stiffness, manufacturability, and modular integration. Components were dimensioned to allow future expansion while maintaining compact packaging.

Frame CAD

Detailed Solidworks CAD modeling of the chassis and swerve module components, optimizing geometry for structural integrity, weight savings, and ease of repair.

Analysis

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) was conducted to validate load paths and ensure that the frame could withstand expected operating forces, including loads that the frame would experience during a crash as to protect the electronics. A combination of Solidworks FEA and ANSYS was used. Solidworks FEA was used to validate and remove any major errors in the design, while ANSYS was used later once the design was refined for higher precision, as my laptop didn't have enough processing power to run ANSYS effectively and quickly.

Finished Assembly

Hardware & Software

Electronics

Designed power distribution and control electronics including motor drivers, microcontrollers, and sensor integration on KICAD. Emphasis placed on modular wiring and expandability.

ROS2

High-level control implemented using ROS2 on a Raspberry Pi, handling motion control, kinematics, and communication between subsystems.
