Modular swerve-drive robotics platform demonstrating mechanical design, structural analysis, and integrated hardware/software systems.

CAD & Structural Analysis

Frame CAD Design

The swerve module frame was designed in SolidWorks with focus on stiffness, modularity, and compact packaging. Component placement allows future upgrades without sacrificing structural integrity, while mounting points simplify assembly and maintenance.

Finite Element Analysis (FEA)

FEA was used to validate load paths and ensure the frame could withstand operating forces and potential crash impacts to protect onboard electronics. SolidWorks FEA helped quickly identify major issues, while ANSYS provided higher-fidelity results once the design was refined for precise stress distribution.

Completed Frame Assembly

The final frame integrates all modules with optimized stiffness and minimal weight. Design supports future upgrades and ensures protection of electronics during collisions or high-load maneuvers.

Hardware & Software

Electronics

Designed modular power distribution and control electronics in KiCad, including motor drivers, sensors, and microcontrollers. Wiring layout supports easy upgrades and ensures reliability for high-speed omnidirectional motion.

ROS2 Control Architecture

Implemented high-level control on a Raspberry Pi using ROS2, handling motion profiling, swerve kinematics, and communication between subsystems. Low-level motor control ran on ESP32 to achieve stable omnidirectional motion at speeds up to 5 m/s while maintaining modularity.

Integrated System

Final assembly includes all electronics, ROS2 software, and frame modules, producing a compact, high-speed, and robust swerve-drive platform ready for testing and further development.
